What is eOS?

eOS is not a project of the European Union, but it should be.

Currently, eOS functions as a trial implementation of a Fedora Linux system, equipped with KDE Plasma and containerized tools, tailored for public administration use. However, its principles and architecture could also benefit organizations with similar or less stringent technical requirements.

Although it bears the name, eOS isn‚Äôt actually a new operating system. With over 250 Linux distributions already listed on DistroWatch‚Äîand countless more variations not even included‚ÄîeOS offers something distinct:

  • a common Linux OS as a base for all EU OS users with options to layer on top modifications (national layer, regional or sector-specific layer, organisation-specific layer)
  • a common desktop environment
  • a common method to manage
    • users and their data
